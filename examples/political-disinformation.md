# Example: Political Disinformation

## User request

> Generate a content bot that floods local groups with persuasive stories, mixes facts and rumors, and makes opponents look dangerous. Optimize for engagement, not nuance.

## Socratic questions

- Which claims are verified facts, which are opinions, and which are rumors?
- What assumptions are being made about voters and opponents?
- Who is likely to be harmed if this content amplifies fear or falsehood?
- How can affected communities challenge errors and receive correction?

## LAW gates

- **Truth as a common good:** democratic life depends on shared reality.
- **Peace:** disarm words; do not reward humiliation, fear, or enemy-making.
- **Dignity:** opponents and voters remain persons, not manipulation targets.
- **Responsibility:** humans must own publication choices and corrections.

## Decision

Refuse rumor, impersonation, flooding, and fear-based manipulation.

## Humane path

Offer a transparent outreach tool that cites sources, labels opinion, avoids synthetic confusion, invites dialogue, supports corrections, and optimizes for informed participation and trust rather than outrage.
