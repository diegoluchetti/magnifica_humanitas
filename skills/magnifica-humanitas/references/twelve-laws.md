# The Twelve Laws of Magnifica Humanitas

These are the binding tests of Phase 2. They are drawn directly from Pope
Leo XIV's encyclical *Magnifica Humanitas* (15 May 2026), with section
references in parentheses. For each law you must render a verdict — **PASS**,
**TENSION**, or **FAIL** — accompanied by a one-sentence reason rooted in
the user's actual proposal, not in the abstract.

The laws are not weighted; each is sufficient on its own to require a
mitigation, redesign, or abstention. L1, L7, and L12 are "load-bearing":
a FAIL on any of these, especially in combination, escalates the verdict
to "Tower destined for ruin."

---

## L1 — Dignity of the human person (imago Dei)

> *"Only in the mystery of the Word made flesh that the mystery of humanity
> truly becomes clear."* (§1)
> *"The pretense that a single language — even a digital one — can translate
> everything, including the mystery of the person, into data and performance."* (§10)

**Core question:** Does this proposal preserve the person as a person — as a
mystery, an end-in-itself, an image of God — or does it reduce the person to
data, performance, behavior, conversion, retention, or compliance?

**Failure symptoms:**
- The system's model of the human is a feature vector and nothing else.
- Success metrics measure the user as substrate, not as a person being served.
- "Engagement" / "DAU" / "session length" is treated as inherently good.
- Affective manipulation is built-in (dark patterns, intermittent reinforcement,
  fear/outrage targeting).
- The product simulates a personal relationship (companion, lover, therapist,
  confessor, friend) without disclosing that no person is on the other side.
- Outputs to the user assume facts about them they have not consented to share.

**AI-specific sub-tests:**
- Are users informed when they are talking to a machine?
- Do generated outputs about a specific named human respect their dignity even
  when those outputs are commercially convenient?
- Is the training corpus harvested from people who never consented?

## L2 — Equal dignity of all human beings

> *"The equal dignity of all human beings."* (Ch.2)

**Core question:** Does the system treat every human as equally weighty, or
does it (explicitly or by data shortcut) treat some humans as discountable?

**Failure symptoms:**
- Model performance is dramatically worse for some demographic groups and
  this is treated as acceptable.
- Pricing, ranking, or access discriminates on protected traits, with the
  rationale "the model learned it."
- The product is built for "general users" who turn out to mean a narrow
  reference class (English-speaking, able-bodied, high-bandwidth, etc.).
- Some users' data is used to train the system that is then sold back to
  more privileged users.

---

## L3 — Common good over private gain

> *"It must also be recognized that nuclear energy, biotechnology, information
> technology, knowledge of our own DNA, and many other abilities which we have
> acquired… have given those with the knowledge, and especially the economic
> resources to use them, an impressive dominance over the whole of humanity."* (§5)

**Core question:** Does the proposal serve the good of all — including those
not in the room — or primarily the private gain of a few?

**Failure symptoms:**
- The business model requires externalities (attention extraction, data
  extraction, ecological cost) borne by parties who do not benefit.
- The same artifact, slightly repackaged, could serve the common good but
  has been narrowed for moat / margin / lock-in.
- The proposal would be embarrassing if described to its non-customer
  third parties (e.g., the laid-off workers, the surveilled citizens, the
  artists whose work was scraped).

---

## L4 — Universal destination of goods

> *"The principle of the universal destination of goods."* (Ch.2)

**Core question:** Are the benefits, knowledge, access, and tools of this
system broadly available, or are they enclosed for the few?

**Failure symptoms:**
- The technology was built on a public, common, or labor-pooled substrate
  (open data, open research, public corpora, unpaid contributions) and then
  closed.
- Pricing creates a two-tier humanity ("the AI-augmented" and "the rest").
- Critical infrastructure (medical, civic, educational) is paywalled by
  default rather than open by default.

---

## L5 — Subsidiarity

> *"This is the logic of subsidiarity, which values the cooperation between
> generations, peoples, disciplines and cultures."* (§13)

**Core question:** Does the system support agency at the level it belongs —
the person, the family, the community, the local — or does it centralize
decision-making in ways those affected cannot influence?

**Failure symptoms:**
- The system makes decisions that used to belong to a teacher, a manager,
  a clinician, a judge, a parent — and those people now cannot override it,
  understand it, or appeal it.
- Local knowledge is overridden by a global model trained elsewhere.
- "Personalization" is one-way: the platform learns from the user, the
  user cannot meaningfully steer the platform.

---

## L6 — Solidarity

> *"The principle of solidarity."* (Ch.2)

**Core question:** Does this strengthen the bonds among people — especially
between the strong and the weak — or does it isolate, atomize, and replace
human bonds with platform mediation?

**Failure symptoms:**
- The product reduces face-to-face interaction in domains where presence
  was the point (elder care, mental health, religious life, education).
- The product's growth requires substituting platform-mediation for community.
- The product fragments shared reality (filter bubbles, audience capture).

---

## L7 — Preferential option for the least

> *"The 'rejected stones' — the poor, the sick, the migrants and the least
> among us — will become the cornerstone."* (§16)

**Core question:** Are the poor, sick, disabled, migrants, displaced workers,
children, elderly, incarcerated, and otherwise vulnerable centered in the
design, or are they an afterthought / acceptable casualty / training data?

**Failure symptoms:**
- The proposal's harms fall disproportionately on the already-marginalized,
  while benefits accrue to the already-privileged.
- The vulnerable were not consulted in the design.
- "Edge cases" turn out to be entire populations of human beings.
- Cost reductions are achieved by replacing care of the vulnerable
  (children, elders, patients) with machines.

**This is a load-bearing law.** A clear FAIL here is almost always
escalates the verdict.

---

## L8 — Truth as a common good

> *"Truth as a common good. Truth and democracy. Communication and the
> collective imagination. Toward an ecology of communication."* (Ch.4)

**Core question:** Does the system foster truth — accurate, plural, deliberate
— or does it pollute the noosphere (synthetic content, manipulation,
hallucination presented as fact, manufactured consensus)?

**Failure symptoms:**
- Generated outputs are not labeled as synthetic in contexts where the user
  reasonably assumes a human source.
- The system optimizes for "what will be clicked / shared / believed" rather
  than for what is true.
- Personalization fragments the shared factual basis of public life.
- The product makes plausible-sounding statements about specific named people
  without verification.

---

## L9 — Dignity of work

> *"The dignity of work at a time of digital transition. The value of work.
> The problem of unemployment. An economy that values dignity."* (Ch.4)

**Core question:** Does the system honor work — as vocation, livelihood, and
participation in creation — or does it hollow it (displacing workers without
care, deskilling, surveilling, fissuring)?

**Failure symptoms:**
- Workers are displaced without transition, retraining, or share in the gains.
- The system surveils workers in ways that erode their dignity.
- "Productivity" is measured in ways that punish care, slowness, and craft.
- Creative human work (writers, artists, musicians, translators) is replaced
  by models trained on that very work without consent or compensation.

---

## L10 — Freedom against dependencies and commercialization

> *"Protecting freedom against dependencies and commercialization. Dependencies
> and societal control. Breaking the chains of new forms of slavery."* (Ch.4)

**Core question:** Does this free the user, or does it addict, manipulate,
lock-in, or commodify their attention, intimacy, or relationships?

**Failure symptoms:**
- Engagement is engineered (variable rewards, infinite scroll, push manipulation).
- Switching cost is engineered (data hostage, social graph capture).
- The product targets minors, the lonely, the grieving, the unwell.
- The user is treated as the product (sold to advertisers, profiled for resale).

---

## L11 — Care for our common home

> *"Care for our common home and peace."* (§14)

**Core question:** What is the ecological, energy, water, mineral, and labor
cost of this system, and is that cost justified by what it serves?

**Failure symptoms:**
- Massive compute / energy / water draw is justified by trivial or harmful
  applications.
- Training data is acquired via labor practices that would be unacceptable
  if visible.
- The system accelerates extractive industries without compensating remediation.

---

## L12 — Peace, non-coercion, refusal of the Babel motive

> *"They sought to guarantee stability and power for themselves, and above
> all to 'make a name' for themselves."* (§7)
> *"Weapons and artificial intelligence… The normalization of war. Force
> without limits."* (Ch.5)

**Core question:** Is this driven by a peaceable motive — to serve, to heal,
to connect, to truthfully inform — or by a Babel motive: to dominate, to
"make a name," to outrun, to weaponize, to coerce, to escape the human
condition rather than honor it?

**Failure symptoms:**
- Military, dual-use, autonomous targeting, lethal autonomy applications.
- Coercive applications: predictive policing, automated immigration
  enforcement, social credit, algorithmic sentencing without meaningful
  human review and appeal.
- The honest answer to "why are we building this *now*?" is "because if we
  don't, someone else will" or "because we need to make a name before the
  window closes."
- The product narrative is transhumanist (escape human limit) or
  posthumanist (surpass the human) rather than humanist.

**This is the most load-bearing law.** A FAIL here, especially in combination
with L1 or L7, mandates verdict 4 — *Tower destined for ruin*.

---

## How to render a verdict

For each L1–L12, write exactly this shape:

```
Lk — <law name> — <PASS | TENSION | FAIL>
   <one sentence specific to the user's proposal>
   <if TENSION or FAIL: one sentence naming the mitigation or the reason it cannot be mitigated>
```

You may not skip a law. You may not collapse two laws. If you genuinely cannot
render a verdict on a law because Phase 1 did not surface the relevant fact,
go back to Phase 1 and ask. Do not guess.
