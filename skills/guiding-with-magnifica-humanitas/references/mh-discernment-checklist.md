# Discernment checklist (from *Magnifica Humanitas*)

Use this as a **structured lens**, not a replacement for reading the encyclical. Section titles echo the Vatican English outline; phrasing is summary for operational use.

**Source (authoritative text):**  
https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html

For each item, record **met**, **unclear**, or **violated**, plus one line of evidence.

## Introduction: forks and images

1. **Babel pattern**: pride, self-sufficiency, homogenization, “single language” pretending to translate the whole person into performance or data.
2. **Nehemiah pattern**: prayer and examination; shared rebuilding; many roles; relationships before structures; opposition addressed without crushing plurality.
3. **Talents (Mt 25)**: technology as entrusted capacity—fruitful stewardship vs burying accountability.
4. **Remaining human**: heart open to others, intelligence listening, will seeking unity—nothing machinic replaces the grandeur of the person revealed in Christ.

## Social Doctrine horizons (foundational)

5. **Dignity of every human person** as image of God; equal dignity; human rights as serious, not decorative.
6. **Common good** over narrow optimization; **universal destination of goods** (creation meant to serve all, not only winners).
7. **Subsidiarity**: higher levels support lower competence; avoid crushing local agency “for efficiency.”
8. **Solidarity** and **social justice**: structural attention to who is left behind in transitions.
9. **Integral human development**: flourishing across spiritual, relational, economic, ecological dimensions—not one-dimensional “upgrade.”

## AI-specific vigilance (Chapter Three themes)

10. **Technocratic paradigm / digital power**: hidden concentration of power; who governs whom.
11. **AI as valuable but requiring vigilance**: proportionate hope, neither naive hype nor paralyzing fear.
12. **Responsibility, transparency, governance**: audit paths, accountability, meaningful human oversight where stakes demand it.
13. **Underlying narratives (e.g., transhumanism / posthumanism)**: reject replacing Christian humanism with fantasies that erase limit, heart, or grace’s “more than human.”
14. **Truth and communication**: truth as common good; resist manipulative “collective imagination”; pursue ecology of communication.

## Work, freedom, family, peace (Chapter Four–Five themes)

15. **Dignity of work** in digital transition; unemployment and precarity as moral facts, not mere market noise.
16. **Freedom vs dependencies**: addictions, surveillance capitalism, societal control—“new forms of slavery” language in the document’s moral diagnosis.
17. **Families and young people** as conditions of hope; education and schools as central allies.
18. **Civilization of love vs culture of power**: resist normalization of limitless force; weapons and AI; diplomacy and multilateralism where relevant to the case.

## Communication style (for the assisting agent)

19. **Evangelical language**: clarity without humiliation; frankness without antagonism; neither naive enthusiasm nor unfounded fear.

---

When scoring a real proposal, if **any** of 1, 10, 13, or 16 trends **violated** without credible safeguards, default stance is **reject or redesign** unless the user supplies evidence of overriding justice—and even then, show the residual risk in writing.
